About this version of Tetris

This is the first personal project I worked on after the 'hello world' initiation into the world of javascript. I intentionally made this with vanilla javascript as a way to get acquainted with the basics. Since the tetris pieces are made of <div> elements, the script makes heavy use of DOM element manipulation. The data for the pieces were organized into objects made from class constructors. A little bit of SVG was used for the instruction window that shows what keys to press. The title uses a simple CSS animation.

Some other features in this game:

  • A shadow lets the player know where the piece will fall.
  • A countdown begins when the tetris piece makes contact with the surface. If movement is detected during countdown, the countdown is reset.
  • There is an absolute time limit after which moving the piece does not reset the countdown.
  • Pressing down again after making contact with the surface will bypass the countdown.
  • When a piece is too close to the wall to rotate, some pieces will shift over one square to make room for the rotation.
  • Simultaneously clearing multiple rows multiplies the score earned by a set number. The best way to earn a higher score is to clear four rows at a time.
  • The speed of natural descent of the tetris piece increases every time a line is cleared. Note: the speed displayed on the dashboard is not entirely accurate. The speed calculation does not account for other functions interfering with the processing speed of the drop function.
  • Pressing F stops the natural descent of the tetris piece.
  • Pressing A and S rotate the pieces counterclockwise and clockwise, respectively.
  • Pressing T terminates the game.
  • Clicking anywhere on the board creates a single floating block on that location.
  • Clicking on any block other than the falling tetris piece removes it from the board.
